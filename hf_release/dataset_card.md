# Dataset card — Synthetic Preventive-Health Longitudinal Instruction Set (v1)

**Draft — do not upload until explicitly asked.**

## Summary

A **fully synthetic** instruction-tuning dataset for longitudinal preventive-health
reasoning. Each record pairs a multi-year synthetic patient timeline with a
structured, non-diagnostic 7-part assessment. Built to fine-tune and evaluate small
open LLMs (Gemma 3 4B, MedGemma 1.5 4B) under QLoRA.

- **No real patient data. No PHI.** Every record is programmatically generated and
  marked `synthetic: true`; the schema hard-fails any record not so marked.
- **Not clinical data**, not derived from any real EHR, and not for clinical use.

## Composition

- 60 synthetic patients, one instruction record each.
- Field schema: `{instruction, input, output, patient_id, synthetic}`.
  - `input` = a compact clinical "flowsheet" rendering of the timeline
    (demographics, family history, medications, immunizations, lifestyle, and a
    3–4 year sequence of labs — HbA1c, fasting glucose, LDL/HDL/triglycerides,
    total cholesterol — and vitals — blood pressure, BMI, weight).
  - `output` = a 7-part assessment: longitudinal summary, risk signals, evidence,
    missing information, clinician questions, safety disclaimer, what-not-to-conclude.
- 5 trajectory archetypes, balanced: cardiometabolic-drift, stable-healthy,
  improving-after-intervention, isolated-hypertension, lipids-plus-family-risk.
- Split by patient (no patient in more than one split): **train 48 / val 6 / test 6**,
  zero patient overlap.

## How it was generated

A seeded Python generator samples a latent trajectory per patient, emits the
timeline, and **derives the gold answer from the same emitted numbers** — so the
gold output is "correct by construction": it only cites values that appear in the
timeline (no hallucinated numbers), and all biomarkers are clamped to the
**preventive / borderline** range so the correct response is always
"monitor + refer", never a diagnosis. Reproducible from `scripts/01`+`scripts/02`.

## Intended use

Research and education only: studying whether medical continued-pretraining helps
preventive longitudinal reasoning, and developing safety-aware evaluation. **Not**
for diagnosis, treatment, triage, or clinical decision support.

## Limitations & biases

- Synthetic: the trajectories are stylized and may not reflect real clinical
  distributions; results on this benchmark do **not** transfer to real patients
  without separate validation on (credentialed) real data.
- Narrow scope: a fixed set of labs/vitals; no imaging, genomics, notes, or
  wearables; preventive/borderline range only (no frank-disease values by design).
- Gold answers are template-derived, so wording diversity is limited.
- Automatic metrics only; no clinician review of the gold answers.
